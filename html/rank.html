Rank Structure

Prerequisites, Duties & Privileges

Carpe Noctem runs on a system of government by representation. If Specialists, Grunts or Recruits, also referred to as the "Community Player Base", would like to propose changes they can relay wishes / suggestions to their assigned JrNCO. It is the JrNCOs duty to speak on behalf of the Community Player Base at the next NCO meeting. The final decision on the direction the community takes is in the hands of the three voting members (SrNCOs) who have equal voting power. SrNCOs must place their voting decisions in the interest of all members and must confer with NCOs and JrNCOs in a monthly meeting before any major decision is made. JrNCOs should argue on behalf of the Community Player Base while SrNCOs represent the needs of management, meta gameplay & business. NCOs are meant to be the neutral tipping point on the scale.

Being part of any branch helps turning the cogs that are running this community. You will have to be at least minimum rank of Grunt to apply for any of the branches. When applying for rank promotions we will consider your investment in branch work.

Community Overview

  • SrNCOs
    • Develop ideas to improve the community
    • Manage the community as a whole
    • Share equal voting rights on final decisions
  • NCOs
    • Supervise assigned JrNCOs
    • Do minor administration work
    • Manage and guide the JrNCOs
  • JrNCOs
    • Represent the Community Player Base in NCO meetings
    • Manage and guide the Community Player Base
  • Community Player Base:
    Specialists, Grunts, Recruits
    • Enjoy the results of branches' work
    • Can contact JrNCOs to relay their concerns or wishes

Basic Responsibilities:

  • Conduct yourself in accordance with our Rules & Regulations.
  • Vote daily for the community on armaclans.com.
  • Reply to event invitations on the website.
  • Be active on the forums.

Recruit [Rec]

Prerequisites:

  • Has filled out an application on the website.
  • Has completed the recruitment interview.

Duties:

  • Basic Responsibilities.
  • Complete the Checklist for Recruits.
  • Does the mandatory reading.
  • Successfully pass Grunt Qualification Form within 6 weeks of joining the community.

Privileges:

  • Can attend events after an Interviewer has confirmed that mods are working properly.

Grunt [Gnt]

Prerequisites:

  • Has successfully completed Grunt Qualification Form - normal case.
  • Is a Carpe Noctem Alumni rejoining the community - rare case.
  • On events has shown esprit de corps.

Duties:

  • Basic Responsibilities.

Privileges:

  • Gets to pick player slots before Recruits.
  • Can vote in official community polls.

Specialist [SPC]

Prerequisites:

  • Has above average proficiency and familiarity with the game.
  • Has above average knowledge of the community organisation.

Duties:

  • Basic Responsibilities.
  • Will take over fireteam leads if asked by HQ.
  • Is online on TS as much as possible.
  • Helps out Recruits whenever they can.
  • Assists players in-game and helps to improve their skills and tactics (point out mistakes in a constructive manner).
  • Provides a short feedback on their experience with Recruits in events.

Privileges:

  • Gets to pick player slots before Recruits & Grunts.
  • Can vote in official community polls.
  • Will be mentored by an assigned JrNCO.

Junior NCO [JrNCO]

Prerequisites:

  • Has completed FTL Training Program.
  • Has the time and the enthusiasm to do organisational work.
  • Is not a flavour-of-the-month.

Duties:

  • Basic Responsibilities.
  • Will take over fireteam leads if asked by HQ.
  • Is online on TS as much as possible.
  • Helps out Grunts & Specialists with their issues.
  • Attends NCO meetings.
  • Is 1IC of an administrative squad.
    • Creates statistics on squad member attendance.
    • Represents squad members issues / suggestions in monthly NCO meetings.
    • Answers questions of squad members.
  • Deals with LoAs and Reserves of administrative squad.
  • Deals with Discharges of administrative squad.

Privileges:

  • Gets to pick player slots before Recruits, Grunts & Specialists.
  • Can vote in official community polls.
  • Can actively shape the community through their work.
  • Will be mentored by an assigned NCO.

Non Commissioned Officer [NCO]

Prerequisites:

  • Has completed SL Training Program.
  • Has the time and the enthusiasm to do organisational work.
  • Is in it for the long haul.
  • Is reliable and dedicated in his work.
  • Agrees to donate $5.- a month for 6 months in advance, amount due: $30.- .

Duties:

  • Basic Responsibilities.
  • Will take over squad leads if asked by HQ.
  • Is online on TS as much as possible.
  • Helps out Grunts, Specialists and JrNCOs with their issues.
  • Attends NCO meetings.
  • Moderates the forums.
  • Supervises assigned JrNCOs.
  • Supervises assigned Specialists.

Privileges:

  • Gets to pick player slots before Recruits, Grunts, Specialists & JrNCOs.
  • Can vote in official community polls.
  • Can actively shape the community through their work.
  • Can demand non-scheduled NCO meetings.

Senior NCO [SrNCO]

Prerequisites:

  • Has completed PltCo Training Program.
  • Has the time and the enthusiasm to do organisational work.
  • Is in it for the long haul.
  • Is reliable and dedicated in his work.
  • Has continuously proven to be an outstanding member of the community.
  • Agrees to donate $10.- a month for 6 months in advance, amount due: $60.- .

Duties:

  • Basic Responsibilities.
  • Will take over platoon command if asked by HQ.
  • Is online on TS as much as possible.
  • Helps out JrNCOs and NCOs with their issues.
  • Attends NCO meetings.
  • Administrates the forums.
  • Supervises all NCOs.
  • Thinks about and thoroughly researches improvements for the community.
  • Makes sure the esprit de corps and corporate identity of the community is not compromised (discharges members if necessary).
  • Counsels with other SrNCOs about developments of the community to get their point of view and feedback.

Privileges:

  • Gets to pick player slots before Recruits, Grunts, Specialists, JrNCOs & NCOs.
  • Can vote in official community polls.
  • Can actively shape the community through their work.
  • Can demand non-scheduled NCO meetings.
  • Can vote on final decisions concerning the communities visions and overall strategy.

Contributor Tag

Prerequisites:

  • Has to be minimum rank of Grunt.
  • Agrees to donate $5.- a month for 6 months in advance, amount due: $30.- (Silver).
  • Agrees to donate $10.- a month for 6 months in advance, amount due: $60.- (Gold).

If a member wishes to donate but can't afford $30.- or $60.- in one payment, we advise to save up until he can make the single payment. This lowers administrative work for the community and eliminates potential scenarios where we have to run after promised donations on a monthly basis. Members can always donate more than $60.- but we can't offer them any additional rewards for doing so.


Privileges:

  • Unlock unique tag on TeamSpeak and the website.
  • Unlock Contributor signature on the forums.
  • Will be named in a "thank you" post on the website.
  • Gain access to cosmetic facewear items in coop events:
    • Any type of half facial bandana / shemagh
    • Any type of full facial balaclava
    • Any type of bandana / glasses mix
  • Gain access to monthly NCO meetings (Gold only)

The Gold Contributors gain access to the NCO meetings in order to observe what their funds are being spent on and how decisions concerning the community are concluded. They are invited to be spectators during these meetings, however some topics will be marked by the meeting leader as "feedback sensitive". On these topics the NCOs will ask for opinions and ideas from present Contributors to get a feeling for the general mood of the community. Attendance to NCO meetings are not mandatory for Contributors, but we do require a response such as attending or "not attending".

Design

Prerequisites:

  • Is proficient enough with picture editing software.
    • And / Or
  • Is proficient with HTML, CSS and Javascript.

Duties:

  • Will work on projects commissioned by the CO or XO for improving the community's visual appeal.

NOTE: One can be both Web Designer and Graphic Designer or can specialize in one of the two.

Interviewing

Prerequisites:

  • Has TeamViewer installed.
  • Has Arma3Sync troubleshooting knowledge.
  • Is online on TS outside of event times as much as possible.
  • Is mature and has good people skills.

Duties:

  • Approaches people waiting in the Help Desk channel asap.
  • Interviews applicants by following the Interview Handbook guidelines.
  • Tests mods with the applicant after he has downloaded them.
  • Continues to help Recruits with their questions as much as possible.
  • Helps with any technical difficulties, e.g. installing the mods.

Public Relations

Prerequisites:

  • Is fluent in speaking and writing English.
  • Is polite and mature in discussions.
  • Is proficient in using internet media.

Duties:

  • Is a "War Photographer", provide awesome screenshots.
  • Is a "War Reporter", writing forum threads about operations.
  • Ensures we get a regular flow of votes on Armaclans page.
  • Maintains the BI forum thread
  • Contacts players looking for squad on BI forums
  • Is active on Reddit Arma page
  • Runs Twitter account

NOTE: It is possible to specialise in one or two bulletins from the list above. Advise with Head of Public Relations for details.

Training

Prerequisites:

  • Is qualified FTL.
  • Is capable teacher.
  • Is mature and has good people skills.
  • Is proficient in Arma 3 mechanics and mod usage.
  • Is proficient with the entire ShackTac Guide.

Duties:

  • Conducts trainings at request of Recruits - BCTs, mod usage etc.
  • Prepares in advance with the training leader for the event.
